Reflecting on Week 9: Relevance & Quality Score

This week focused on relevance, quality score, and Google Analytics. Before this, I thought quality score was just another number that Google gives you. After working through everything, I realized it actually plays a big role in how well your ads perform. It depends on things like using relevant keywords, writing helpful ads, and making sure your landing page matches what people are searching for.

One thing that really stood out to me was how everything in a Google Ads campaign works together. It isn't enough to just write a good ad. Your keywords, ad copy, and landing page all have to match if you want people to have a good experience. The more relevant everything is, the better your quality score can be, and that makes a lot more sense now.

We also learned how to install Google Analytics, and that was probably my favorite part of the week. It was really cool being able to connect it to my cubing timer website and actually see what was happening in real time. At first, I could see that nobody was on the site. Then, when I opened my website in another tab, I watched it update and show that someone was online. It was a simple thing, but seeing the live data made Google Analytics feel a lot more useful and made everything we learned seem more real.

Overall, this week helped me understand that digital marketing is more than just creating ads. It's about making sure everything works together so people can easily find what they're looking for. I think these ideas will definitely help when I continue improving my own website.
