Blog Post

This week gave me a better sense of how much strategy actually goes into online business, especially when it comes to getting people to find your site in the first place. One of the main things I worked with was Google Ads and Keyword Planner, and it was interesting to see how much research goes into something as simple as choosing the right words. I used to think ads were mostly just about paying to show up, but now I see that it really depends on understanding what people are actually searching for and how to match that with what you're offering.

Beyond the tools themselves, I also started thinking more about the bigger picture of running an online business. It's not just about getting traffic, but also about what happens after someone clicks on your site. Things like having a clear message, making the website easy to use, and building trust all matter just as much as getting the visitor in the first place. If people feel confused or unsure, they'll leave quickly no matter how they got there.

Overall, this week helped me realize that online business is a mix of data, creativity, and patience. The tools like Google Ads are helpful, but they only work well when they;re part of a bigger plan that focuses on real people and what they actually need.
