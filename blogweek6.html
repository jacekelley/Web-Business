Blog Post

This week focused on legal structures, licenses, and taxes for online businesses. One of the topics I found most interesting was learning about partnerships. Before this week, I had not really thought much about how different business structures affect ownership and responsibility. It was interesting to see how partnerships allow multiple people to share the work of running a business, while also sharing profits and liabilities. Learning about the advantages and disadvantages of different legal structures helped me better understand what business owners need to consider before getting started.

We also learned about licenses and taxes. Something that surprised me was how many legal requirements can vary depending on the type of business and where it operates. Even small businesses may need permits, licenses, or tax registrations before they can legally operate. While these topics can seem complicated at first, I can see why they are important for protecting both business owners and customers.

As I continued working on my speedcubing website, I thought about how these lessons apply to my own project. Since I am a web development major and my goal is to share information with the speedcubing community rather than sell products, I decided not to include any payment systems on my website. I think keeping the site focused on resources, tutorials, and community content makes the most sense for my goals. Overall, this week helped me better understand the legal side of running an online business and gave me a better appreciation for the planning that happens behind the scenes.
