Reflecting on Week 10: ROI & Optimizing Ad Performance

This week focused on ROI, conversion tracking, and optimizing ad performance. Before this week, I mostly thought that once you created a Google Ads campaign, there wasn't much left to do besides let it run. After going through the lessons, I realized that there's a lot you can do to improve how your ads perform by looking at the data and making changes over time.

One of the biggest things I learned was how important it is to track conversions and measure whether your campaign is actually accomplishing its goal. Instead of only looking at clicks or impressions, it makes more sense to focus on whether people are taking the actions you want after visiting your website. That gave me a much better understanding of what ROI really means and why marketers pay so much attention to it.

My favorite part of the week was finally unpausing my Google Ads campaign and letting it run. Since then, it's been really cool to check in on it every now and then and see visitors showing up on my cubing timer website from different parts of the world. Watching real people find and use something I built has made the whole project feel much more real than just looking at numbers on a screen.

Overall, this week showed me that creating an ad is only the beginning. The real work comes from paying attention to how it's performing, making improvements, and learning from the results. It's been fun seeing my campaign in action, and I'm looking forward to continuing to optimize the website and see how it performs over time.
