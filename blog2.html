Blog Post

This week helped me better understand the different ways businesses can source products for an online store. Before this module, I did not realize how many options there were beyond just making products yourself. Learning about brokers, manufacturers, wholesalers, affiliate marketing, and drop shipping showed me that every business model has its own advantages and challenges depending on the goals of the company.

One thing that stood out to me was how important relationships and communication are when working with suppliers or manufacturers. A business depends heavily on the people providing the products, so trust, reliability, and product quality all become major factors in long-term success. I also learned that wholesalers can make it easier to buy products in bulk, while brokers can help connect businesses with suppliers they may not have found on their own.

The discussions about affiliate marketing and drop shipping were also interesting because they showed how someone can start an online business with lower upfront costs. At the same time, these models can be more competitive since many sellers may be offering similar products. It made me realize that even if the startup costs are lower, businesses still need strong marketing, customer service, and branding to stand out.

Overall, this week taught me that sourcing products is one of the most important parts of building a successful online business. The way a company chooses to source its products can affect pricing, customer satisfaction, shipping speed, and overall growth. It also showed me that running an online business requires planning and flexibility because every sourcing method comes with trade-offs that need to be carefully considered.
