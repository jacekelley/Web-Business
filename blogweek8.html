Reflecting on Week 8: Ads & Ad Groups

This week was all about getting under the hood of Google Ads. Honestly, before starting these assignments, I mostly thought of ads as just "the stuff I usually scroll past." But actually sitting down to build out campaigns and organize ad groups really changed my perspective on how much work happens behind the scenes.

The biggest "aha!" moment for me was learning how to structure ad groups. I realized it’s not just about throwing a bunch of keywords into a bucket and hoping for the best. It’s actually more like a logic puzzle—you have to make sure your keywords, your ad copy, and the user's intent all line up perfectly. Seeing how specific you have to be to make sure an ad actually makes sense to the person searching for it was a huge eye-opener.

Writing the copy itself was surprisingly tricky, too. You have a very limited amount of space to grab someone's attention, and it forced me to be way more intentional with my word choices. It wasn't just about sounding "salesy"—it was about trying to predict exactly what someone is looking for and giving it to them immediately. It made me realize that successful advertising is really just about understanding human behavior and being helpful at the right time.

Looking at my speedcubing site, I’m already thinking about how I could apply this. Even if I’m not running paid ads tomorrow, the whole idea of "targeting" is going to change how I write my content. It’s all about connecting the right information to the right person. This week definitely took the mystery out of digital marketing for me, and I’m feeling a lot more confident about how these systems actually work.
