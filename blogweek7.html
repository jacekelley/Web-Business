Blog Post

This week focused on Google Ads and learning how businesses use keywords to reach potential customers. One of the most valuable parts of the week was creating our own Google Ads campaigns. Even though we did not actually launch the campaigns, it was helpful to work through the process and see how much planning goes into online advertising before an ad is ever shown to users.

A major topic this week was keyword research. Before this assignment, I knew that keywords were important for search engines, but I did not realize how much strategy is involved in choosing the right ones for an advertising campaign. Businesses have to think about what their customers are searching for and which keywords are most likely to attract the right audience. I found it interesting to see how a small change in keyword selection can affect the effectiveness of an entire campaign.

Creating the Google Ads campaign also gave me a better understanding of digital marketing. Writing ad copy, organizing ad groups, and selecting keywords required more thought than I expected. It showed me that successful advertising is not just about making an ad look appealing. It also involves understanding customer behavior and making decisions based on research and data.

As I continue working on my speedcubing website, I can see how these concepts could be useful in the future. My current goal is to provide resources and information for the speedcubing community, but if I ever wanted to expand the site's reach, understanding Google Ads and keyword targeting could help bring more visitors to the website. Overall, this week gave me a better understanding of online advertising and showed me how important keywords are in connecting businesses with the people they want to reach.
