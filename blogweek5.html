Blog Post

This week we spent a lot of time learning about commerce and ecommerce businesses, especially how websites can be used to sell products, accept payments, and interact with customers online. We talked about how many businesses use payment systems so customers can safely buy products or services directly from a website. Even though payment systems are important for many ecommerce businesses, I decided that I do not want people to pay for my speedcubing website. Instead, I want my website to focus more on sharing information, helping users learn, and creating a fun experience for the speedcubing community.

Another thing I decided is that I want my website to have multiple pages instead of just one page. I think having different pages will make the website more organized and easier for users to navigate. For example, I could have a homepage, a page with speedcubing resources, and another page for tutorials or personal best times. I also want users to be able to choose between a few preset themes so they can customize how the website looks. Giving users different themes could make the website feel more interactive and personal. Overall, this week helped me better understand how ecommerce websites are designed and how I can apply some of those ideas while still keeping my own website simple and focused on the speedcubing community.
